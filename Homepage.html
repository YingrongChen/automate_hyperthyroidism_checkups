Automate Hyperthyroidism Checkup

Input My Thyroid Data

Intro: digital medicine in thyroidology

Digitized medical information, such as height, weight, blood pressure, pulse rate, and blood test results, has emerged as a highly promising field and changed the landscape of disease prediction, prevention, diagnosis, treatment, and post-treatment management.

Hyperthyroidism is a chronic condition when patients’ overactive thyroids produce too much thyroxine hormones. After being diagnosed, patients will take anti-thyroid drugs and recheck their thyroid hormone levels regularly to determine an appropriate amount of medication. Besides, hyperthyroidism often relapses after the initial treatment. I believe digital science will make monitoring hyperthyroidism easier. The Thyroid Function Test (TFT) usually give patients data of certain hormone levels along with suggested ranges recognized as normal/too much/too little. Besides, hyperthyroidism is closely related to easily collectable medical data like heart rate and weight. I want to develop a program that can take in the blood test results as well as the patients’ heart rate and weight, and then check the progress or regress of hyperthyroidism. This is particularly useful to avoid overdose during treatment period and check for disease recurrence in the remission period after treatment. With helps from professional endocrinologists, this program may give suggestion in whether to change the prescriptions (although in the early-stage patients should still follow their doctors’ guidelines). Similar programs can be built for other hormone diseases or chronic conditions that have relatively simple checkups after diagnosis or initial treatment.
